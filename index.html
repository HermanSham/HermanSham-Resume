Herman Sham

Mechanical Engineering

Hello! My name is Herman Sham and I am currently a junior studying Mechanical Engineering at University of California Riverside. I have a strong passion for engineering and have gained hands-on experience through various projects and coursework. I have worked on projects involving CAD design, robotics/electronics, and cost benefit analysis. In addition, I have experience using softwares including SOLIDWORKS, FUSION360, MATLAB and MS OFFICE. I have completed coursework in areas such as fluid dynamics, thermodynamics, and computational systems. I am also actively involved in extracurricular activities such as my school's robotics club, where I lead a small team in building a combat robot from scratch.

I have always been fascinated by the way mechanical systems work and the challenges of designing and improving them. I have a strong interest in problem-solving and critical thinking, and I believe that mechanical engineering is the perfect field to apply these skills. Additionally, I am passionate about making a positive impact on the world through my work, and I believe that mechanical engineering offers many opportunities to do just that. Throughout my studies, I have had the opportunity to work on various projects that solidified my decision to pursue mechanical engineering. For example, I worked on a team to design and build a theoretical drone-delivery package receiving device for rural areas, which allowed me to apply my knowledge of mechanical systems to make a real-world impact.

One of the projects I have worked on involved coding using MATLAB. This project involved the analysis of thermal insulation tiles on space shuttles undergoing shear and compressive stresses during the ascent of the shuttle. When temperatures begin to decrease, the aluminum skin of the shuttle contracts, causing the tiles that are attached to the skin to compress into each other. With given values of temperature change, lengths of tiles, and other variables, we could find the exact shear and compressive stresses of each tile. I also implemented a GUI for ease of use for future users of my code. Some personal projects I have worked on involve using SOLIDWORKS to create CAD designs of regular gym eqquipment and weightlifting machines.
